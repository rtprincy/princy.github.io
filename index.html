About Me

I am a joint-PhD candidate in Astronomy at Radboud University and KU Leuven. My research is dedicated to advancing the understanding of the formation and evolution of hot sub-luminous stars by leveraging multiband time-series data and machine learning methodologies.

Current Projects

Project 1: Unsupervised machine learning for variable stars identification and characterisation

The aim of this project is to leverage machine learning techniques to characterise the variablity of hot subdwarfs (evolved compact stars) observed from the Gaia mission. The t-distributed stochastic neighbor embedding (t-SNE) dimensionality reduction algorithm was used to visualise high-dimensional feature space (mainly Gaia DR3 lightcurve statistical parameters) in a low-dimensional feature space, resulting in distinctive clusters in the t-SNE 2-D embeddings. This helps in discovering diverse types of new variables, which are crucial for understanding their formation and evolution.

Project 2: Mode identification using amplitude ratio

This project aims to explore multi-band pulsational amplitudes of pulsating hot subdwarfs to identify pulsation frequency modes through the amplitude ratio techniques.
